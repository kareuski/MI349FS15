    • The Benefits of Different Yoga Poses

    With each yoga pose there come many benefits from each pose that you embrace. Here is a guide that can help you remember how different asanas (poses) benefit you.

    Mountain Pose

    Mountain pose is one of the most important standing poses in yoga practice. Standing poses are the instrument to improving posture. Moutain pose is the basic start to any yoga routine as it gives you the proper alignment of your feet and body.

    Forward Fold

    Forward bends strech your lower back, release tension in your upper body and can help in spinal flexibility.

    Cross-Legged Pose

    Seated poses are ones that make you feel calm and will make you find your true inner peace. Cross-legged pose helps in increasing flexibility in your lower back and hip flexors. Seated postures help in improving back muscles for a strong and healthy posture.

    Balance Poses

    Balance poses will help you develop coordination, aiding in toning your muscles. This also means they can also help in improving your posture by elongating your spine in order to keep your balance. Fun fact: there are more than just physical benefits from the balance poses! These types of poses help you with your focus. Paying attention is the key component in the proper form of any balance pose.

    Back Poses

    Back poses help release tension in your limbs and back as well as strengthen them. These poses stretch out your muscles, can help in opening your hips sockets and increase the mobility of your spine.

    Stomach Poses

    Along with back poses, stomach poses help relieve tension and also stregthen your spine. They also help stregthen your arms as well as increase flexibility in your hip region.

    Inverted Poses

    There are many inverted poses to choose from however, the most popular combination would be the headstand followed by the plow. These poses help develop strength in your upper body while stimulating your brain with focus. These poses are all incorporated by strong core and spine strength. Beginners can start by practicing a headstand. When your bring your legs higher than your heart it relieves tension in your legs due to the reverse flow of blood. The plow followed after the headstand will help relieve any back tension you could have.

    Corpse Pose

    AHHH! The best and most important pose of them all, final savasana. This poses will calm your mind, body, and soul collectively as one which will give you a deep feeling of total relaxation. This pose can be used before your practice as well as finish it off. This pose may seem easy however, relaxing poses are the most challenging because of our inability to fully relax. Practice your corpse pose and challenge yourself to quiet the mind.
